I could simply tell you what email services are, but instead I'll show you what and how to evaluate whether an email service is private and secure or not:
1 - Open source.
2 - Encryption types.
3 - Support for independent encryption mechanisms, such as PGP/GPG.
4 - Read the terms and conditions of the service.
5 - See the legal history of the service. 

Precautions:
1 - Always use a VPN (P2P) on a virtual machine.

Recommended email services based on my evaluation:
Riseup:
Created by activists for activists, with the purpose of being a private and secure email against state surveillance and censorship.
Does not use GPG natively, but allows manually.

Tutanota: 	
It has its own encryption algorithm. 
The encryption keys are generated locally by the device.
It does not have native GPG support.
follows german privacy laws.
Offers 1 GB of inbox.
Open Source.
Support for encrypted calendars and contacts.


ProtonEmail:
Based on strict swiss data protection laws (LGPD).
Support for GPG encryption.
Open source.
500mb inbox.
Send email for to other recipients who do not use Proton email, using symmetric encryption.
